Konstantinos in (1) paragraph

Ι was born in Athens, Greece, in 1985. I hold a Bsc in Statistics from the Athens University of Economic and Business - AUEB, a MSc in Biostatistics from the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens - UOA and a PhD in Biostatistcs (Clinical trials - Meta-analysis) from the University of Utrecht. Between 2011-2013 I was affiliated with the Hellenic Center of Disease and Control Prevention center where, among other tasks, I focused on resetting the National Public health registry. During my PhD, I conducted research on statistical methods for the synthesis of rare diseases clinical trial data within the Asterix project (EU-FP7 2013-2017). I have worked in or collaborated with university medical centers in Greece, The Netherlands, Germany and the United Kingdom. I am currently affiliated with the University Medical Center Utrecht (UMCU), Department of Biostatistics and the University of Thessaly (UTH), Department of Public Health. I teach (Bayesian) biostatistics and programming in UTH and AUEB.
